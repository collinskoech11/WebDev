    • HISTORY

    Makina Baptist Church began in 1987 as a common cell-group by Missionary Lynn Raburn and Pastor James Muhanji along Kabarnet road.

    It later shifted to Kibera,Makina near Kibera law courts in 1988.

    Unfortunately the Kibera Law Courts wanted to expound on its space and the church had already grown therefore it was allocated its current location in Kichinjio Area (it was called so because there was a cattle slaughter house by then) by then Chief Kosgei.

    Pastor James Muhanji and Pastor Shivoka with other church members joined hands to construct the church at its present location in 1989

    Then Missionary Raburn departed hence leading to arrival of Missionary Hopper who aided in construction of the church that is currently up with the leadership of Pastor Walter Matete who is currently serving at First Landmark Baptist Church,Satellite

    Pastor Matete served untill 1994

    During the leadership of Pastor Matete the church began a Sunday School for the kids and later on it grew to a Primary School which operated untill 2019 where it collapsed.

    Pastor Matete was taken over by Pastor Evans Jumba who served from 1995 to 2008

    Pastor Evans Jumba lead to growth of cellgroups which were basically for visiting each other and encouraging brethren

    The cell-groups are:

    Mashimoni led by Joseph Shisanya and Joseph Wakhu

    Makina led by John Ingosi and Joseph Mahira

    Lindi/ Kisumu Ndogo led by Nicholas Mutambi and Julius Aswani

    Olympics led by Charles Obes and Mabel Indasi

    During his time Pastor Evans Jumba and other church members sent Missionary John Wanyonyi to Southern Sudan,Kapoeta with a mission of starting churches and christianity to the youngest country

    This was a success since it lead to growth of Churches in Kapoeta,Nachikut and others

    Later Pastor Elijah Mahindu took the leadership from 2008 to 2012

    Under the leadership of Pastor Elijah Mahindu the First youth camp was hosted in Makina Baptist Church in 2011, August 28th with Patrick Abuti as the youth leader.

    The camp had 11 churches

    Mission Work

    Makina Missionary Baptist Church has alot of missionaries who either operates internally or outside the country.

    This work is led by Brother Charles Obes as the Mission Work Co-ordinator.

    He is the one who gives reports about mission work and any aid from the sister churches

    Missionary John Wanyonyi was the first one to be sent outside the country by the church in 2009

    His mission work has been a success since it led to creation of more churches and conversion of more people to christianity.

    Also it has enabled youths from Southern Sudan to get a chance of higher Education in Kenya

    For internal missionaries, It led to the beginning of Mathare Baptist Church which is lead by Preacher Joseph Maira

    There is also another one in Gatwekera which was initial started by Silanga Baptist but later revived by Makina Baptist led by Brother Joseph Wakhu
